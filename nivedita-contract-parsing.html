When Flex Can Claim

A clause-by-clause reading of the Flex manufacturing & supply agreement — every mechanism that lets Flex adjust price or recover cost, and the exact event that triggers the right to claim.

Source: Nivedita — Contract Parsing Perspective: Flex (manufacturer / EMS) Counterparty: Customer Categories parsed: 27

This agreement is built so that Flex's margin is insulated from cost shocks it doesn't control. The 27 clause categories below fall into seven recurring themes. For each, two things matter: the mechanism that protects Flex, and the trigger — the specific event that activates Flex's right to charge, adjust, or recover. Each row is tagged by the kind of claim it creates.

01

Pricing & Fee Adjustments

7 categories

Levers that let the agreed price or fees move when an external cost — materials, FX, tax, scope — shifts underneath Flex.

Category & TypeProtection / MechanismRight-to-claim Trigger
Change Order ManagementPrice Adjustment
Allows pricing/schedule adjustments and reimbursement of implementation costs for engineering changes. Protects margin if changes increase cost.
When the parties agree to an engineering change and its impacts; Flex may claim implementation costs and adjusted Product pricing tied to the change.
Fees; Price ReviewPrice Adjustment
Quarterly review permits price adjustments for increases in fuels, materials, labor, utilities, and other production costs demonstrably impacting Flex.
When Flex demonstrates increased underlying costs during quarterly review; parties to agree (consent not unreasonably withheld/delayed).
Additional Fees / CostsPrice Adjustment
Customer liable for costs arising from specification/volume/assumption changes, Governmental Change, Customer-controlled material issues, pre-approved expediting, and unfavorable material cost increases outside Flex's control.
When any listed event occurs (e.g., forecast/volume changes, Governmental Change, AVL issues, expediting, supply constraints).
Currency & Exchange RatesPrice Adjustment
Quarterly FX adjustment and true-up via debit/credit memos to reflect >±0.75% FX movement and actual non-invoice currency conversion impacts.
When quarterly FX fluctuation exceeds ±0.75% and/or when quarterly true-up shows differences from the reference rate.
TaxesPrice Adjustment
Customer is responsible for taxes, duties, tariffs, customs charges, brokers' fees, and similar governmental charges on the Products/Services; these amounts are in addition to base prices.
When Products or Services are subject to applicable taxes, duties, tariffs, or related charges; Flex may invoice these as separate pass-throughs.
Excluded ChargesCost Recovery
Certain cost categories are expressly excluded from base prices — CAPEX, NRE, Launch, Ramp, Underutilization — and are recoverable separately per the Agreement's specific mechanisms for each item.
When any excluded cost category is incurred or becomes applicable under its governing clause (e.g., CAPEX recovery schedule/true-up, NRE/Launch/Ramp charges, quarterly Underutilization true-up).
Miscellaneous ServicesCost Recovery
If scope/fees are not pre-agreed but Customer instructs performance, Flex may invoice quoted charges.
When Customer engages or instructs Flex to perform Miscellaneous Services without finalized scope/fees.
02

Capital, Startup & Capacity

4 categories

Recovery of the big up-front and capacity-related investments Flex makes to serve the Customer — with end-of-period true-ups so nothing is left stranded.

Category & TypeProtection / MechanismRight-to-claim Trigger
CAPEXCost Recovery
Customer must fund CAPEX via lump sum, installments, or price uplift; includes a 10% charge and end-of-period true-up for unrecovered CAPEX.
When CAPEX is incurred/paid; if recovered via pricing, a claim for any unrecovered balance is triggered at the end of the defined payback period.
Startup Charges (NRE, Launch, Ramp)Cost Recovery
Customer reimburses NRE/Launch/Ramp (plus 10% charge) via lump sum, installments, or price uplift; overages (not due to Flex fault) are chargeable; end-of-period true-up for unrecovered amounts.
When such costs are incurred and/or exceed quoted amounts; if recovered via pricing, Flex may claim any unrecovered balance at the end of the defined period.
Underutilization of CapacityCost Recovery
Quarterly review/true-up of Underutilization Costs when actual purchases fall below Forecast or capacity is lost due to Customer decisions.
When Customer's purchases are below Forecast or capacity is lost due to Customer actions (including CCM non-availability).
Overutilization of CapacityCost Recovery
If CAPEX cannot meet demand, Customer must either fund additional CAPEX or reduce demand.
When Customer's demand exceeds current CAPEX capability.
03

Inventory & Materials Risk

6 categories

The largest cluster: every path by which inventory Flex buys for the Customer becomes the Customer's financial responsibility rather than stranded on Flex's books.

Category & TypeProtection / MechanismRight-to-claim Trigger
Customer Controlled Materials (CCM)Risk Transfer
If Customer's CCM terms add cost or restrict procurement, parties must compensate Flex or align terms; Customer must match Flex payment terms for CCM.
When CCM terms cause additional cost/risk, shorter payment terms, or procurement constraints; Flex may claim compensation or require term alignment.
Customer Responsibility for InventoryCost Recovery
Customer is responsible for Inventory Flex purchases for Customer's Products; protects Flex from stranded inventory carrying risk.
When Inventory is procured to support the Products under the Agreement.
Excess, Aged & Obsolete Inventory (E&O)Risk Transfer
Monthly E&O process: Customer validates and must scrap, cash deposit, or purchase E&O; Net 0 terms for shipped E&O; additional costs for non-compliant changes.
When E&O is reported/validated; Customer must instruct scrap, fund deposit, and/or purchase; charges also trigger for unapproved changes causing reschedules/cancellations.
Quantity / Schedule ChangesRisk Transfer
Customer liable for extra costs to meet increases or reschedules; delays attributable to Customer are treated as reschedules with extra costs.
When Flex agrees to quantity increases or reschedules, or delays are Customer-caused; Flex may claim incremental costs.
CancellationsRisk Transfer
Customer cannot cancel without approval; if cancelled, Customer must pay for NCNR materials, vendor cancellations, Underutilization, Termination Charges, Monthly Charges; must buy finished/partial Products after 30 days.
When Customer cancels or fails to pick up on time; charges calculated from the first day after cancellation.
Exhibit 2 — Inventory Turnover ModelCost Recovery
Working Capital Advance framework: Initial Advance, monthly reporting, and cash settlement up/down based on Actual vs. Budgeted Inventory; termination reconciliation applies.
On monthly review periods when Actual Inventory fluctuates vs. Budgeted ITO; at termination, Customer purchases inventory and the Working Capital Advance is applied/settled.
04

Payment, Credit & Collections

2 categories

Enforcement levers that protect Flex's cash position when the Customer is late or its credit risk rises.

Category & TypeProtection / MechanismRight-to-claim Trigger
Late Payment RemediesRemedy
1.5% per month interest on late payments; Flex may stop work, demand prepayment, delay shipments, and charge storage, restart, vendor penalties and other costs arising from non-payment.
When Customer misses payment deadlines or Flex reasonably believes Customer's credit risk has increased; charges accrue while remedies are exercised.
Credit Terms / Security InterestRemedy
Flex may reduce credit limits or require LOC/escrow to cover exposure (inventory and A/R). While protective, non-compliance can cause suspensions and carrying costs.
When credit reviews warrant tighter terms; claims relate to conditions for continued performance and potential drawdown to cover exposure.
05

Delivery, Acceptance & Warranty

3 categories

How cost and risk are allocated at the moment of shipment, return, and warranty — mostly shifting freight, "no-defect" returns, and exclusions back to the Customer.

Category & TypeProtection / MechanismRight-to-claim Trigger
Shipping TermsRisk Transfer
EXW; Customer pays freight/insurance/special packing not in the original quote.
On shipment per Incoterms; when special packing beyond quote is required.
Product AcceptanceRisk Transfer
Customer bears costs and risks for returns with "no defect found."
When returned products are evaluated and no defect is found.
Express Limited WarrantyRemedy
Flex must repair/replace or refund for workmanship defects within 12 months and pay return freight; many exclusions shift costs to Customer (e.g., CCM, design, misuse, prototypes).
When a Product fails the limited warranty within the warranty period and is returned with RMA; exclusions trigger Customer liability instead.
06

Termination, Liability & Indemnity

4 categories

What happens at the end — and the liability boundaries. Note the two clauses that work against full recovery: the damages waiver and the liability cap.

Category & TypeProtection / MechanismRight-to-claim Trigger
Effect of Expiration / TerminationCost Recovery
Customer must pay for Inventory at termination/expiration; WIP converted to finished Products and sold at then-current prices; Termination Charges apply.
Upon termination/expiration; Flex may claim Termination Charges, inventory purchase, and WIP conversion recovery.
Flex IndemnificationRemedy
Flex indemnifies Customer for third-party claims tied to Flex-selected processes/Production Materials causing IP infringement, certain environmental non-compliance, or injuries caused by Flex's warranty breach.
When qualifying third-party claims arise; the duty to defend/indemnify attaches per the notice and control provisions.
Exclusion of Certain DamagesRemedy
Waiver of consequential and other categories of damages can limit Flex's recovery for Customer-caused harm, creating potential net unrecovered costs.
When damages are of excluded types; Flex's recovery is limited despite incurring real costs.
Cap on LiabilityRemedy
Aggregate liability cap (with carve-outs) can prevent full recovery of Flex's losses, leading to leakage if costs exceed the cap.
When total recoverable damages exceed the agreed cap (except for listed carve-outs).

Compliance

1 category

Compliance costs beyond the baseline are pushed to whoever asked for them.

Category & TypeProtection / MechanismRight-to-claim Trigger
Code of Conduct (additional requirements)Risk Transfer
Customer bears costs for Customer-requested additional compliance requirements.

The throughline. Read top to bottom, the agreement is a single idea expressed 27 ways: Flex prices and performs against a forecast, and almost any deviation the Customer causes — a change, a shortfall, a cancellation, a late payment, stranded inventory — converts into a chargeable event. The only clauses pointing the other way are the damages waiver and the liability cap in Theme 06, which bound what Flex itself can recover. This page is a structural reading of the parsed clauses, not legal advice — defer to the executed contract for exact terms, thresholds, and carve-outs.
